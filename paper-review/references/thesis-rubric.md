# thesis review rubric

Use this checklist for master's theses, dissertations, thesis chapters, final submission reviews, and advisor feedback integration.

## core checks

- research question or objective is explicit, answerable, and revisited in the conclusion
- title, abstract, introduction, contributions, results, discussion, and conclusion describe the same work at compatible levels of strength
- contributions are concrete, defensible, and supported by the manuscript
- chapter order and transitions build a clear argument rather than collecting loosely related sections
- terminology, notation, abbreviations, datasets, methods, and metrics stay consistent across chapters
- limitations are explicit, proportionate to the evidence, and reflected in the final claims

## content checks

- literature review synthesizes prior work into themes and makes the research gap sharper
- claims about prior work are cited, fairly stated, and connected to the thesis contribution
- chosen methods are justified against the research question
- datasets, preprocessing, implementation details, parameters, software versions, hardware, prompts, or other reproduction details are stated where relevant
- evaluation uses appropriate metrics, baselines, ablations, statistical tests, or comparisons where relevant
- figures, tables, captions, and text agree numerically and conceptually
- results answer the research question without overstated causal or general claims
- ethical, legal, privacy, bias, and validity concerns are addressed when relevant

## final submission checks

- no visible placeholders, unresolved references, or drafting comments remain
- figures and tables are referenced, captioned, legible, and discussed
- citations, labels, cross-references, bibliography entries, appendices, and acronyms are complete and consistent
- abstract can stand alone and agrees with the final results
- appendices are referenced from the main text and support the thesis
- acknowledgements, declarations, title page, and institutional formatting requirements are complete if required

## advisor feedback checks

- convert each feedback item into one or more concrete edits
- group edits by chapter or file
- mark unclear feedback as a question instead of guessing
- identify feedback that conflicts with other instructions or thesis constraints
- preserve high-priority technical corrections ahead of local wording preferences
